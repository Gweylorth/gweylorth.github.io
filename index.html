About Me

Geek, Gamer, Metalhead... Sure, why not? I do like to spend hours exploring the vast plains of Hyrule, preparing and mastering quests for a group of daring adventurers, unfolding the devious plans of evil warlocks, reading the adventures of Rincewind across the Discworld and headbanging at Apocalyptica concerts.

No, I am not doing all of these most of the time, at least not at the same time, since I currently am studying Computer Engineering at INSA Rennes, section Languages, Systems and Networks.

PyCubic

Practical Studies team project at INSA Rennes - 2012/2013

PyCubic is a Python interface for cubic graphs manipulation, developed for mathematicians working with cubic graphs theory.
PyCubic is able to display, manipulate and edit cubic graphs, by opening graph6 or GraphML files. You can create, save and load graph layouts, or just apply some layout algorithms to compute an automated representation of the graph. Then, you can save your edited graphs in graph6 or graphML format, and export them as PDF, PS, SVG and PNG files.
For code samples, check the main PyCubic class, the graph actions handler, or one of our converters.

  • Team : 2 students
  • Date : May 2013 (9 months)
  • Techs : Python 2.7, GTK+ 3 with PyGObject, Glade3 and Git

SWOP - SmallWorld of POO

Software Engineering School Project - INSA Rennes - 2013

Welcome to the ruthless world of SWOP! SWOP is a turn-based strategy game, playable alone or with another player, on a single computer (hot seat) or through network. Find your opponent (be it human or AI), choose your faction and fight for victory!
Check the User Manual out (in french) to have a glimpse of what the game looks like. The code consists mainly in C# and WPF, with a touch of C++ for map generation and tile suggestions, and was written with Visual Studio 2012.
Since it was a project for two, and we enjoyed working together, we both participated to almost everything in the game. However, I tended to focus on software architecture, and gameplay programming.
We added much more than what was asked for and tried to make it visually appealing as well. To see a few code samples, try the classes Faction, LocalGame, or Unit.
  • Team : 2 students
  • Date : December 2013 (4 months)
  • Techs : C#, C++, WPF, UML, Visual Studio 2012 and Git
